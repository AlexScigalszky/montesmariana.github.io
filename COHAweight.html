Separability measures

Next to the links to the clouds, there are some links titled Semvar, where you can find semvar code output (and the code, in a jupyter notebook) looking at separability measures for clouds. The measures are taken for the distance matrices both before and after dimensionality reduction; there are global and local measures (with different k); the classes analyzed are lemmas (for clouds of all types) or mbroad (for clouds of single types), broadGenre and newBroadGenre.


Click to show description of current clouds.

These clouds represent the similarity between tokens of state, country and church, either with all types at once, one at a time or only two combined.

The tokens come from the Semcor corpus. The ppmi values for first order weighting come from COHA. The threshold indicated in the columns of the tables was applied to this weighting. After applying the threshold, tokens that had lost all the weighted context words were dropped.

The second-order co-occurrence (SOCC) ppmi values come from COHA. '6k' indicates that the token vectors have 5795 features (the same as the clouds in the main index, with the intersect), while '10k' indicates that they have 10000 features (the 10 thousand most frequent contentwords from COHA).

Now you're ready to enjoy the clouds. Please beware of saturation risk by looking at them for too long. Belgium has plenty of real clouds if you need to refresh your eyes.
